# Introducing AI Into a Team Workflow

AI adoption works best when it starts small, stays grounded in real work, and builds trust through visible wins.

This playbook outlines a practical way to introduce AI into a workflow without overwhelming teams.

---

## Step 1: Start with one workflow, not a whole function

Choose a workflow that:
- happens frequently
- has clear pain points
- produces measurable outputs
- has a natural place for human review

Avoid starting with the most complex, high-stakes process.

---

## Step 2: Define the job to be done

Be specific about:
- what the system should help with
- what it should not do
- what success looks like for the team

If the job is unclear, the system will feel noisy instead of helpful.

---

## Step 3: Design for trust, not just performance

Trust is earned when teams can see:
- what the system used as context
- why it produced an output
- where human judgment matters

If people cannot understand it, they will not adopt it.

---

## Step 4: Pilot with a small group

Pick a small user group with:
- curiosity
- influence
- willingness to give feedback

Treat the pilot as learning, not a final rollout.

---

## Step 5: Build feedback into the workflow

Feedback should be:
- quick to provide
- easy to categorize
- reviewed regularly

The fastest way to kill adoption is to collect feedback and ignore it.

---

## Step 6: Measure adoption signals

Look beyond accuracy.

Track:
- repeat usage
- time saved
- reduction in rework
- user confidence
- workflow completion speed

These are the signals that tell you whether AI is actually helping.

---

## Step 7: Scale only after the workflow feels stable

Scaling too early creates noise.

Scale when:
- users trust the system
- the workflow is clear
- guardrails are working
- failure modes are understood

AI adoption is a capability, not a launch.
